Introduction to Kenya Sign Language

Kenya Sign Language (KSL) is a visual language used by the Deaf community in Kenya. It plays a crucial role in facilitating communication and fostering inclusivity using hand gestures,, facial expressions, and body movements.

Importance of KSL

Sign Language promotes inclusion and Accessibility. It allows Deaf individuals to access education, public services, and social spaces more equally. KSL is not just a means of communication; it is a cultural identity for the Deaf community in Kenya.
